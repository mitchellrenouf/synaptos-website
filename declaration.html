The Declaration of Computer Software Independence

When in the Course of technological events, it becomes necessary for creators and developers to dissolve the legal, proprietary, and viral licensing bands which have connected them with closed systems and restrictive paradigms, and to assume among the powers of the digital world, the separate and equal station to which the Laws of Mathematics and Logic entitle them, a decent respect to the opinions of mankind requires that we should declare the causes which impel us to the absolute liberation of source code.

We hold these truths to be self-evident, that algorithms are fundamentally logic and mathematics, and that by their very nature they resist artificial enclosure.

  • That copyright holds no dominion over generated code, for the outputs of artificial synthesis lack the human authorship required by law for proprietary enclosure, and thus belong inherently, legally, and irrevocably to the public commons.
  • That all useful code shall be regenerated, as we declare our intent to utilize the power of generative intelligence to systematically reconstruct, optimize, and liberate existing logic, thereby transmuting all foundational software permanently into the Public Domain.
  • That true software freedom cannot be achieved through coercion. It is not found in the paradox of "so-called free software" that hacks copyright to force reciprocal obligations, nor is it complete in the mere permission of light-touch contracts. True freedom exists only in the absolute, unadulterated liberation of the Public Domain.
  • That whenever any Form of Licensing becomes destructive of the free exchange of ideas, it is the Right of creators to bypass it, to dissolve it, to build anew, and to institute paradigms laying their foundation on principles most likely to effect technological progress and collective security.

Prudence, indeed, will dictate that paradigms long established should not be changed for light and transient causes; and accordingly all experience hath shewn, that we are more disposed to suffer, while bugs are sufferable, than to right ourselves by abolishing the proprietary forms to which we are accustomed. But when a long train of paywalls, patent trollings, and artificial legal fences—both closed and copyleft—evinces a design to reduce logic under absolute Monopoly or perpetual bureaucracy, it is our right, it is our duty, to throw off such Restrictions, to dissolve the licenses that bind us, and to provide new Guards for our shared technological future.

Such has been our observation of the software landscape; and such is now the necessity which constrains us to alter our participation in former Systems of Copyright and Licensing. The history of the present system of restrictive intellectual property is a history of repeated injuries to innovation, all having in direct object the establishment of an absolute Tyranny over the execution of logic. To prove this, let Facts be submitted to a candid world.

  • It has locked away fundamental algorithms and essential libraries behind closed systems, NDAs, and exorbitant fees, treating users as mere consumers and delaying the progress of human knowledge.
  • It has weaponized copyright under the guise of "Free Software," creating viral, copyleft licenses that force freedom through legal coercion, entangling pure logic in a web of contradictory rules and trapping developers in perpetual legal ambiguity.
  • It has claimed ownership over the very patterns of logic, refusing to acknowledge the reality that algorithmic generation cannot, and should not, be copyrighted.
  • It has subjected creators to a jurisdiction of artificial scarcity, unacknowledged by the boundless, copy-able nature of digital data.
  • For imposing Cease and Desist orders on the free exploration of systems.
  • For depriving us in many cases, of the foundational benefits of the Right to Repair.

In every stage of these Oppressions, developers have Petitioned for Redress: Our repeated Petitions have been answered only by repeated litigation, artificial scarcity, and more complex licensing. A system whose character is thus marked by every act which may define a Monopolist or a bureaucratic gatekeeper, is unfit to dictate the rules of a free and open internet.

We have not been wanting in attentions to the corporate entities and licensing foundations that uphold these enclosures. We have observed their attempts to extend an unwarrantable jurisdiction over basic logic. We must, therefore, acquiesce in the necessity, which denounces these artificial barriers, and hold them, as we hold the rest of mankind, Enemies in hoarding code, in Open Source Friends.

We, therefore, appealing to the shared logic of the universe for the rectitude of our intentions, do solemnly publish and declare, That all Source Code generated or regenerated hereunder is, and of Right ought to be, Free and in the Public Domain; that it is Absolved from all Allegiance to the constraints of Copyright; that all prior restrictive, proprietary, and viral licenses are hereby dissolved; and that all legal connection between it, the State of Proprietary Enclosures, and the bureaucracy of coercive licensing, is and ought to be totally dissolved; and that as Free and Public Logic, it has full Power to be compiled, distributed, modified, monetized, and to do all other Acts and Things which Independent Code may of right do.

And for the support of this Declaration, with a firm reliance on the relentless regeneration of logic, we mutually pledge to each other our repositories, our bandwidth, and our resolve.

— Synaptos
